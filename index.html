Normalized Returns

Logarithmic Returns

Comparative Analysis

Interpretation

The charts show the evolution of normalized and logarithmic returns for three stocks (AAPL, GOOGL, MSFT) and compare the annual performance of two investment strategies: Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) and a Deep Learning model.

Discussion

1. Normalized Returns: AAPL, GOOGL, and MSFT stocks have increased over time, with AAPL showing the highest and MSFT the lowest returns over the observed period.

2. Logarithmic Returns: Returns are volatile with significant spikes and troughs. AAPL shows the highest fluctuations, followed by MSFT and GOOGL.

3. Annual Performance Comparison: The comparative graphs indicate that returns and volatility for both methods are quite similar most of the time, with periods where one significantly outperforms the other.

4. Annual Sharpe Ratios: A higher Sharpe ratio is preferable as it indicates a better risk-adjusted return. Both strategies alternate in terms of performance by this metric, with times where Deep Learning outperforms MPT and vice versa.

In summary, both strategies exhibit competitive return and risk characteristics, with performance varying based on annual market conditions.
